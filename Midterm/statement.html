Design Rationale

The visual and interaction design for this Martinique destination site follows a restrained, professional aesthetic that supports both academic presentation and practical trip planning. Typeface selection pairs a humanist serif (Lora) for body text with a geometric sans (Montserrat) for headings; this combination provides legibility for extended reading while conveying contemporary refinement in headings and CTAs.

Color choices—Celadon (soft green), deep teal for accents, and a warm sand tone—were selected to reflect the island’s tropical vegetation, Caribbean sea, and sunlit beaches. This palette is accessible and calming; high-contrast accents (coral) are reserved for CTAs (Book Now, Request Info) to meet usability and visual hierarchy best practices.

Text placement prioritizes scanability: short paragraphs, descriptive headings, and card components to present activities and restaurants. The hero image is prominent to immediately establish place identity. Buttons are placed near descriptive text and at the top of primary pages for quick action—this helps users convert while remaining informed.

The site also reflects personal context: Martinique is my father's family origin and, despite this personal tie, I have not yet visited. The design therefore aims to balance ethnographic respect (clear sourcing, recommended local businesses) with aspirational imagery that invites first-hand exploration.
