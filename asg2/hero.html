Hela

The Asgardian Goddess of Death

Overview

Hela is a fictional character appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. She is based on the Norse goddess Hel and first appeared in Journey into Mystery #102, created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby. Hela rules over Hel and Niflheim, where she governs the spirits of the dead who are not honored in Valhalla.

In the Marvel Cinematic Universe, Hela made her live-action debut in Thor: Ragnarok (2017), portrayed by Cate Blanchett. She is depicted as the older sister of Thor and Loki, returning to reclaim Asgard after Odin’s death. Blanchett’s performance received widespread acclaim, bringing a darker and more menacing portrayal of the character.

Hela is known for her immense powers, including superhuman strength, necromancy, and the ability to conjure weapons. She commands an army of the dead and has long sought to expand her reign beyond Hel, often clashing with Odin, Thor, and other Asgardians. Her ambition and ruthless nature make her one of Thor’s most formidable adversaries.
