Six Thinking Hats

Summary

The Six Thinking Hats method, developed by Edward de Bono, is a structured way of looking at problems from multiple perspectives. Each hat represents a unique thinking styleâ€”facts, emotions, caution, optimism, creativity, and management. By separating these modes, groups can think more clearly and make better decisions.

The Hats

White Hat: Information

Focuses on facts, data, and information available. Questions include: What do we know? What do we need to know?

Red Hat: Emotion

Expresses feelings, hunches, and intuition. Allows emotions to be part of decision-making without needing justification.

Black Hat: Judgment

Cautions against risks, weaknesses, and potential problems. Encourages critical thinking and logical caution.

Yellow Hat: Optimism

Highlights benefits, value, and opportunities. Looks for why something could succeed and its positive impact.

Green Hat: Creativity

Generates new ideas, alternatives, and possibilities. Encourages different approaches to problem solving.

Blue Hat: Management

Focuses on process control, organization, and decision-making. Keeps discussion structured and guides next steps.
