Six Thinking Hats Reflection

The White Hat – Information & Facts

My partner and I began by focusing on factual details about my semester project. We reviewed the project’s goals, available data, and user research to identify the gaps in information that could be filled. This helped us clarify what data supports my current design direction and what additional research would improve accuracy and user understanding.

The Red Hat – Emotions & Intuition

When we put on the Red Hat, emotions and instincts came into play. We discussed how users might feel when interacting with the interface — whether it evokes trust, curiosity, or frustration. My partner noted that visual warmth and tone can heavily influence perception, reminding me that emotional design is as powerful as functionality.

The Black Hat – Critical Thinking

Under the Black Hat, we explored potential flaws and weaknesses in my design. My partner challenged the efficiency of certain navigation choices and accessibility standards. I personally relate to the Black Hat the most, since I naturally focus on identifying risks and areas for improvement to ensure a stronger, more reliable user experience.

The Yellow Hat – Optimism & Benefits

Wearing the Yellow Hat, we identified the project’s most positive aspects. My partner appreciated the clarity of the design layout and the engaging color scheme. We concluded that the project’s potential to enhance user satisfaction and simplify navigation stood out as its strongest value.

The Green Hat – Creativity & New Ideas

The Green Hat conversation centered on innovation. We brainstormed creative enhancements such as micro-interactions, user onboarding animations, and expanded accessibility features. These ideas opened new possibilities for engagement and gave the project a refreshed sense of energy and growth.

The Blue Hat – Organization & Control

With the Blue Hat, we focused on management and structure. We established a clearer workflow for project milestones, refining how tasks are tracked and evaluated. This discussion helped define priorities and ensured that the project remains consistent and cohesive through completion.

Reflection – Project Evolution

After the Six Thinking Hats discussion, my project evolved significantly. By combining analytical, emotional, and creative perspectives, I refined both the interface and research strategy. The dialogue emphasized the importance of balancing optimism with realism, and organization with experimentation. This process strengthened my confidence in iterative design and encouraged me to pursue a more holistic approach to problem-solving.
